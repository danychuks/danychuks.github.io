## Research Interests

My research interests are in frame theory, dynamical sampling, and their applications to signal processing. I am interested in understanding how signals or elements of a Hilbert space can be sampled, represented, and reconstructed in stable ways.

In particular, I am interested in dynamical sampling problems, where the data are obtained from repeated measurements of an evolving system. A central question is how to recover an unknown initial state from samples taken at different times.

I am also interested in fusion frames, Gabor frames, scalable frames, weighted frames, and the role of oblique projections in frame theory. These ideas are useful in signal processing, distributed sensing, and reconstruction problems.

## Current Research Direction

My current research direction focuses on extending dynamical sampling theory to settings involving unbounded linear operators. This is motivated by evolution systems that arise naturally in analysis and applications, where the governing operator may not be bounded.

I am interested in questions related to stability, reconstruction, and frame conditions in this setting.

## Keywords

Frame theory, dynamical sampling, fusion frames, Gabor frames, scalable frames, weighted frames, oblique projections, signal processing.
